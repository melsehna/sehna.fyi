Computational biology · Evolution · Phenotyping

Second year computational biology PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University working on bacterial behavior, evolution, and large-scale phenotyping.

I study how microbial populations make decisions, adapt to stress, and evolve under spatial and environmental constraints, using a mix of modeling, image-based phenotyping, and data analysis.

This site is a place for research summaries, ongoing projects, and technical notes.
